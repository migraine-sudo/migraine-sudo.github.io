About ​

Security researcher focused on IoT, vehicle connectivity, and mobile security. I pay special attention to risks that have direct real-world impact—e.g., abuse of vehicle control links, or theft and fraud in payment scenarios. My work is practical and engineering-driven: build reproducible attack chains to verify the true risk boundary, pinpoint root causes, propose actionable fixes, and drive vendors through remediation and retesting.

I’m currently exploring AI for Security: how to use AI to boost security research and defense efficiency. The goal is to make frontier technology serve a reliable and controllable real world.

Goals ​

  • Drive real fixes with offense/defense: use reproducible attacks to validate real risks, and push vendors to fix and retest.
  • Make intelligence safer: as AI permeates vehicles, homes, and critical systems, study new attack surfaces and verifiable security baselines.
  • Build with long-term responsibility: practice responsible disclosure and long-termism, keep doing useful security research so more people benefit from technology.
